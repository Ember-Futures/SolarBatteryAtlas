Solar + Battery Atlas

How the tool works, and the data behind it.

The Goal: Can solar panels plus battery storage, on their own, deliver constant round-the-clock "baseload" power 24/7/365 — and at what cost? This tool tests that question at thousands of locations worldwide.

How it works

  • Everything is sized per 1 MW of constant, round-the-clock demand, so results scale to any load. You choose how much solar (MW per MW of demand) and battery (MWh per MW of demand) to build.
  • We start from a full year of hourly weather data (2024) from NASA POWER for each location.
  • For each hour, the local sunlight and temperature are turned into the DC solar output that the chosen solar build would produce.
  • An hour-by-hour simulation then dispatches that energy: solar meets demand directly when the sun is up; surplus charges the battery (accounting for round-trip losses); the battery discharges to cover shortfalls; whatever is still unmet is recorded as a gap.
  • Across the whole year, this gives each location's Capacity Factor. It is pre-computed offline for 19 solar/battery combinations per location and loaded into your browser, so panning the map is instant.
  • LCOE is calculated live in your browser from those capacity factors and your own cost assumptions — change the costs, financing, or year and results update immediately.

Key Metrics

  • Capacity Factor (CF): the share of the year that solar + battery keep the baseload fully supplied — the reliability of the system.
  • LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy): the average lifetime cost per MWh, accounting for up-front build cost (CAPEX), operating costs (OPEX), financing (WACC), panel degradation, and equipment lifetimes. You can switch between global cost assumptions and local, region-specific costs, roll costs forward to a future year, and optionally add a diesel backup generator to firm up the last few percent of supply.

What you can explore

  • Capacity Factor Map: how reliably a chosen solar + battery build can meet the load, everywhere.
  • Sample Weeks: replay a representative week hour-by-hour to watch solar output, battery charging/discharging, and any unmet demand.
  • Potential Map: how much solar energy each area could realistically generate (PVOUT), under technical land limits or stricter policy limits.
  • LCOE Map: the lowest cost to hit a target reliability at each location; compare against a reference location and see the breakeven cost of moving power between them.
  • Supply-Demand Matching: line up where solar + storage works against where people live, how much electricity they use, grid reliability and access, and the fossil plants it could displace.

Data Sources

Solar & Weather Data: NASA POWER (Prediction of Worldwide Energy Resources)

Population Data: NASA Socioeconomic Data and Applications Center (SEDAC), Gridded Population of the World (GPW), v4

Electricity Consumption Data: Chen, J., Gao, M., Cheng, S., Hou, W., Song, M., Liu, X., Liu, Y., & Shan, Y. (2022). Global 1 km × 1 km gridded revised real gross domestic product and electricity consumption during 1992-2019 based on calibrated nighttime light data. Scientific Data, 9, 202. https://doi.org/10.6084/m9.figshare.17004523

Power Plant Data: Global Energy Monitor, Global Coal Plant Tracker, Global Oil & Gas Plant Tracker, Global Bioenergy Plant Tracker, Global Nuclear Plant Tracker, December 2024 release. Data available under Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License.

Solar Potential (PVOUT): Global Solar Atlas (World Bank Group / Solargis). Used in the Potential Map to estimate realistic annual generation per area.

Grid Reliability & Electricity Access: HREA (High Resolution Electricity Access), used in the Supply-Demand Matching demand layers.

Cost of Capital (WACC): Country-level WACC from Hatton et al. (2025), Scientific Data 12, 1912. Local mode assigns the nearest available country WACC per Voronoi cell.

Local CAPEX Assumptions: Regional solar/battery CAPEX assumptions from IRENA/IEA/BNEF. Local mode maps each Voronoi cell to a region and interpolates 2024/2035/2050 values by year.

Diesel Prices: Retail diesel pump prices (World Bank / GlobalPetrolPrices), clamped to a global unsubsidized floor. Used for the optional diesel-backup comparison.
